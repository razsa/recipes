Ingredients:
3 medium italian eggplants
3 medium cloves of garlic, minced
2 tablespoons juice from 1 lemon, plus more as desired
3 tablespoons tahini 1/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley leaves 
kosher salt

directions:
1.preheat a gas or coal grill to medium heat and place eggplants directly over hear source.
Cook, turning occasionally with tongs, until completely tender and well charred on all sides,
30 to 40 minutes. wrap with foil and let rest for 15 minutes. 

2. open foil package. working one eggplant at a time, use a sharp paring knife to slit open lengthwise.
carefully scoop out soft flesh with a large spoon and transfer to a strainer set in a large bowl. 
once all eggpalnt is scooped, pick out any stray bits of skin and blackened flesh and discard.

3. transfer eggplant to a salid spinner, distributing it evenly around the perimeter. spin gently
 until all excess moisture is extracted. discard all drippings, wipe out bowl, and return eggplant to bowl.

4. add garlic and lemon juice to eggplant and stir vigorously with a fork until eggplant breaks down into
a rough paste, about 1 1/2 minuts. Stirring constantly and vigorously, add the tahini followed by the olive
oil in a thin, steady stream. the mixtire should become pale and creamy. stir in parsley and season to taste
with salt and more lemon if desired.

5. transfer to a serving bowl, drizzle with olive oil, and server with warm pita bread or vegetables for
dipping. baba ganoush can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to four days. let
baba ganoush ware to room temperature before serving.
